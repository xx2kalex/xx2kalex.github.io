There are two main qualities of a 'bad' website in my opinion, these being a lack of responsiveness and ambiguity in terms of finding what I need. A lack of responsiveness in a website is a productivity killer to the user because they are forced to spend extra time on the website to complete a task that should otherwise be completed in a more efficient manner. One example that I could think of for this was the nba.com website a few years ago. Although after just checking it now it does not seem to be a problem anymore, it used to be very slow pulling up box scores of recent games and essentially any data-heavy information that was not on the landing page. This was a problem because it turned users like me away from using that website as my go-to source for searching up stats of recent games.

The other problem, ambiguity in terms of finding what I need is an issue because it also causes me to spend more time and thus energy on finding what I need, which can deter users away from a website. An example that I found is lingscars.com, a car leasing website based in the UK. This website is extremely busy and just by looking at it, it is impossible to distinguish one thing from another without spending extra energy to look at it closely. That is an obvious problem that would deter users.
